Things To Do

One of the finest ways to discover 'The White City' is to do so the old-fashioned way: on foot, on a free walking tour!The Arequipa Walking Tours are led by qualified and licensed tour guides who are fluent in both Spanish and English. During the trip, you will see some of the city’s most architecturally significant and significant buildings.

The Arequipa Walking Tour takes you to a variety of important historical sites in the city, as well as markets and stores that are distinctive to Arequipa. You will see the first hotel and jail in Arequipa, the alpaca zoo – where you can pet, feed, and observe how alpaca fur is transformed into a variety of apparel – and the San Camilo Market. Here you can find subterranean food courts, butchers, native spices, fruit booths, and fresh fish! Throughout the day, this walking tour is offered twice. The first is at 10:00 a.m., while the second is at 15:00 p.m. Depending on the time you choose, the tour’s path differs slightly.

The Arequipa Walking Tour itineray may change slightly based on the time of year and weather. During your tour you will see many of the same locations listed above, but could also see:

1.) The Andean Sanctuary Museum – A museum dedicated to the frozen, preserved body of a young Inca girl, sacrificed to the gods on the extinct Ampato Volcano.

2.) Barrio del Solar - A residential area of Arequipa that is home to the Bolognesi Bridge. This bridge is the oldest in the city and offers a fantastic view of the surrounding volcanoes as well as the river rushing by below.

These are free tours, there is no need to book in advance. All you have to do is show up and you’re ready to go!
